Part 6
- The discussion started with a reference to previous proposals for principles of disclosure and regulation, specifically in relation to defy disclosure and KYC seed when using five protocols.
- The focus then shifted to stable coins and their decentralized nature. It was noted that the value of stable coins is increasingly being dictated by the user experience, rather than a centralized system.
- The potential issues with this transition were acknowledged, including the lack of transparency and accountability in some stable coin systems. This was exemplified by the lunar collapse and the need for robust systems to check what backs the stable coins and creates the yield.
- The non-financial applications of stable coins were then discussed, particularly how identity and access control can influence the value of these coins. This was likened to complementary currencies used in fairs or carnivals, where the value is controlled by the entity that controls the supply.
- It was proposed that there could be different types of stable coins, each with different methods of backing and regulation. For instance, some stable coins might be backed by transparent or pseudo-transparent banking reserves, while others might be specific to certain identities.
- The discussion concluded with a note that the issues around stable coins will be covered in a series of reports, rather than a single comprehensive paper. The role of identity in stable coin regulation was identified as a key issue to be explored in these reports.

Part 7
- Although Bitcoin could be used as a payment tool by some participants but not by many, stable coins can be used by many.
- The discussion focused on the stability of stable coins and their potential use by many, even though they might include some interest earnings.
- The possibility of discussing the KYC issue in depth in conjunction with the activities of the IKP working group was raised.
- The value that can be provided by attributing identity within these systems was acknowledged.
- The potential for a stable coin with a direct relationship to contributing data was raised.
- The impact of a legalized global stable coin, primarily denominated in the US dollar, on payment instruments for trades, capital markets, and commodities was acknowledged.
- The current USDC or USDT does not necessarily require KYC, but they have the function to halt or freeze the movement of the coins by the sanctions address.
- It was suggested to discuss the ID issues not only as a word but also as the financial word used in financial transactions.
- The USDC vendor redemption to US dollars or business KYC was identified as a way to have some level of AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and CDF (Combating the Financing of Terrorism) in the system.
